ABOUT ME

As a GIS graduate student at South Dakota State University, my research focuses on the integration of Artificial Intelligence and geospatial data to extract meaningful trends, insights, and robust decision making in disaster response and management. My goal is to generate predictive models that can analyze temporal data and real-time information to forecast the probability and severity of future disaster and generate hazard maps for situational awareness. Additionally, utilize geospatial big data through Social Sensing to obtain time-sensitive, disaster-associated data and analysis in aim to enhance timely disaster response.

I hope to continue with my career in GIS, Remote Sensing, Data Science. I am passionate about the emerging trends in GIScience and laveraging Artificial Intelligence in Geospatial Data Science. My focus is to enhance my GeoAI and Data Science knowledge, R, and Python programming skills.

Apart from my academic and career endeavours, I am a social and outspoken individual. I enjoy cultural music/dances, karaoke, nature photography, watching wrestling, and a strong Arsenal supporter.
